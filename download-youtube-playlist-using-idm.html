How to Download a YouTube Playlist Using IDM

Learn how to quickly download entire YouTube playlists using IDM and a free online tool.

By Ashutosh Debnath September 25, 2025 Windows, YouTube

If you have installed IDM and added its extension to Chrome, you can download individual videos from a playlist using the IDM download button, which appears at the top-right corner of the video. However, this process can be time-consuming.

To make downloading faster and more efficient, we will use an online tool called YouTubePlaylist.cc to generate a download link for the entire playlist. This link can then be used in IDM to download all the playlist videos automatically.

DISCLAIMER & WARNING:

Before we get started, please be aware that downloading copyrighted content without proper authorization may infringe on YouTube’s terms of service . Make sure you have the necessary permissions or are downloading content that is allowed for offline viewing.

IMPORTANT:

This method is only applicable for playlists that are created by channel owners and consist of videos from their own channel.

Step #1: Generate Download Link for the Playlist

To generate the download link, we will use YouTubePlaylist.cc, a freemium online tool that allows free users to download up to 15 videos per day.

Since YouTubePlaylist.cc uses the playlist URL to create the download link, start by copying the URL of the playlist you want to download. Open the playlist in any web browser, highlight the URL by double-clicking it, and press Ctrl + C to copy the link.

Once you have copied the link, visit YouTubePlaylist.cc and click the Sign Up button to create a free account.

TIP:

Use temporary email addresses to create multiple accounts. This allows you to download 15 videos per account until youve downloaded all the videos you need.

After signing up, paste the playlist URL into the “Enter URL” field. The tool will display all available videos for download from the playlist.

To select specific videos, hold Ctrl and click each video with the mouse. Youll see the number of selected files displayed at the bottom right corner.

To set video quality, click on the Set Quality/Subtitle option at the bottom right corner. A pop-up window will appear with quality options.

In the pop-up, click Auto Quality to open a list of available resolutions. Choose your preferred video quality, then close the pop-up window.

Click Download All at the bottom right corner. The tool will start processing the selected videos. Wait for it to complete.

Once the videos are ready, click the “Export Link” button. A pop-up window will appear with the download link.

In the pop-up, click Copy to Clipboard to copy the download link.

A “Copied” message will appear to confirm that the link has been copied to the clipboard.

In the next step with IDM, we’ll use this link to download the videos.

Step #2: Use IDM to Download Videos

Now that you have the download link, its time to open IDM and add the videos to your download list to start downloading.

To open IDM, use the Windows built-in search feature: Press “Win + S” on your keyboard, type “IDM” in the search field, and click on “Internet Download Manager” under the Best match section.

In IDM, click on the “Tasks” tab, then select “Add batch download from clipboard” to use the download link copied from the youtubeplaylist.cc tool.

A pop-up window will display all available videos for download.

Click “Check All” to select all videos, or tick the individual checkboxes next to each video title to select specific videos as needed. Then, click OK.

A small pop-up window will appear in the bottom-right corner, prompting you to choose a queue (using a queue allows you to pause, resume, and easily manage your downloads).

I recommend creating a new queue for easier management. To create a new queue, click the “+” icon.

Another pop-up window will ask you to name the queue. You can choose any name, but for this guide, lets call it “lets-find-and-fix.” Click “OK” when done.

After naming the queue, a scheduler window will open. Close it to return to the queue selection pop-up.

Here, youll see the newly created queue (“lets-find-and-fix”) already selected.

Tick the “Start queue processing” option and click OK.

Now, all selected videos will start downloading.

Step #3: Keep Track of Your Downloads

To monitor the download status of your videos, follow these steps to access the queue you assigned during the download setup.

Expand the “Queues” section in the “Categories” menu on the left-hand side. Click on the name of the queue you wish to view.

On the right-hand panel, youll see the download progress for each video in that selected queue.

Step #4: Locate Downloaded Videos on Your System

If youre unsure where IDM has saved the files, you can check the default download location in IDM.

Heres how:

In the menu bar, click on “Downloads,” then select “Options” from the context menu. This will open the IDM configuration window.

In the Configuration window, go to the “Save To” tab, then select “Video” as the category from the dropdown menu. Youll see the path in the “Default download directory for Video category” field.

To easily access this folder, start by copying its path.

Highlight the default download location by clicking and dragging your mouse across it, then right-click and select “Copy” (or press Ctrl + C on your keyboard).

Now, open File Explorer (press “Win + E” for quick access). In the address bar, paste the copied path and press Enter.

This will take you directly to the folder where your downloaded videos are stored.

This is it. By following these steps, you can download any YouTube playlist using IDM and enjoy your favourite videos offline without any hassle.
